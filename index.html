The Big Picture

A guidance to peace engagement through expanded consciousness

The big picture thinking is simply the expansion of consciousness, and the usage of that expanded consciousness for strategic growth and development. Every society today faces both unique and challenging circumstances that inhibit the level of growth and development needed to match the era we are living in.

While the world is moving fast, and we see the global population increasing at a fast rate the rifts between individuals and groups have only widened. At the center of it all is inequality. Other factors like the global pandemic, food shortage and security will always highlight the shortcomings of each society.

"At this juncture, a matching type of leadership is required to take on these modern day inefficiencies, taking into consideration at the same time, the local, regional and global geopolitical ramifications. Bold leaders, giving more, dignifying others, showing empathy and being courageous."

Over twenty years, U.S. Global Peace Department monitored, conducted in-depth study and research strategies that can be used to steer societies through these times. Sustained progress is only achievable with leaders who learn, understand and demonstrate the audacity to see the Big Picture.

This Big Picture skill-set includes a range of areas like peace building, reasonable governance, conflict resolution, emotional intelligence, geopolitical clarity, strategic out-sight, accountability and people cohesion.

Our Mission

Promoting the peace department concept globally

Leadership and Authority

The head of this department would be a secretary or cabinet level appointment whose sole role would be to provide to the President and government a peace path for all engagements locally, regionally and internationally.

Department Structure

Built out like a foreign service with a local bureau for domestic issues. This department would have a corresponding unit to engage along with its foreign service counterpart.

Funding

The department would receive direct annual funding from the national budget for all its activity domestic and abroad.

Expanded Sub Units

The Peace Department shall establish units within every department and agency, including entities of the government i.e schools, and use these units as satellite for intervention and continuous training.
